Supporting Malala Fund

Malala Fund and Cochlear Foundation have joined forces to raise awareness of the needs of millions of girls, children and young
people with hearing loss worldwide. Together, we will promote their equal rights to an education and early access to hearing
treatment.

Early access to better hearing can help children speak, learn, and develop, significantly improving their ability to gain an education
and realise their full potential. 1,2.

However, worldwide 34 million children have disabling hearing loss. 3 If not hearing loss is not treated early on, children often
experience a slow progression throughout their academic life, including lower school performance, higher risk of dropping out of
school, and less likelihood of accessing higher education4. Ultimately, this can hamper their lifelong career prospects.3

This problem is exacerbated in many parts of the world where children – especially girls – already face significant barriers to
education. 5


Promoting equal rights to education for girls, children and young people with hearing loss


At age 15, Malala Yousafazi was attacked by the Taliban for speaking out for girls’ education. In 2013 she co-founded Malala Fund
with her father, Ziauddin. A year later, Malala received the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of her efforts to see every girl complete
12 years of free, safe, quality education. The fund continues to champion this mission.

Malala who has personal experience of hearing loss and has a cochlear implant added her voice to the hearing health cause by writing
a personal foreword for the World Health Organization’s first World Report on Hearing in 2021. In the report she said that with all the
barriers to equality facing girls, women and other marginalized people, those who need hearing care are further disadvantaged and
too often left behind.

Three paths that lead to change


Throughout our three-year partnership, Malala Fund and Cochlear Foundation we aspire to remove hearing loss as a barrier to life’s
opportunities – ensuring girls, children and young people can gain an education and realise their ability to thrive throughout their life.
To achieve this, we will:

1. Parents: Appeal to parents to check and if needed, treat their child’s hearing loss from early childhood to make sure hearing loss
doesn’t hamper their personal development, ability to access education and realise their full potential.

2. Hearing health community: Join forces with hearing health non-government organisations (NGOs) and advocacy organisations to
collectively raise awareness of the needs of millions of children with hearing loss worldwide. We’ll promote their equal rights to an
education and early access to hearing treatment.

3. Governments and societies: Support a growing global call for governments and societies to prioritise hearing health, providing
children access to the most appropriate hearing treatments so they can speak, learn, develop and realise their full potential.

Just as Malala inspires people everywhere through her amazing story of courage and persistence, we will work alongside Malala Fund
to inspire girls, children and young people with hearing loss, encouraging them to gain an education, build self-esteem and realise
their full potential throughout their life.
